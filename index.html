My Story

I didn't get a clean start.
I built one.

I moved out on my own after leaving a difficult home situation. No plan, no cushion, just the decision that I was done waiting for things to get better.

I dropped out of high school. I'm not hiding it. I've been turned away from entry-level jobs, cashier positions, mall retail, customer service roles, because I don't have a diploma. I've sat across from hiring managers and watched them pass over me for something I don't have on paper.

"Rejection taught me more than those jobs ever would have."

So I found work that doesn't ask for credentials: construction. I show up every day, put in the hours, and keep the lights on. That's the foundation. And while I'm on the job site, I'm also building something else.

What I'm Building

Hands in the dirt.

Right now, my life runs on two tracks at once. I'm not waiting for the right moment. I'm creating it.
